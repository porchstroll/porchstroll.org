Questions, answered plainly

The things people actually ask — including the skeptical ones. If a question isn't here, ask us.

We'd rather answer the hard questions ourselves than let them go unanswered. Where something isn't settled yet, we say so.

The structure
Are you really a nonprofit? Are you a 501(c)(3)?

porchstroll is being incorporated as a Maryland nonstock nonprofit corporation. We are not currently a 501(c)(3) — that's a tax-exemption status, and we've chosen not to pursue it for now. The mission protection we care about comes from the nonprofit corporate form itself: no shareholders, and a legal rule against distributing surplus to insiders.

More on the reasoning is on the main page and the governance page.

If you're a nonprofit, how do you make money?

Nonprofit means no owners profit — not that no money changes hands. For-profit businesses pay a subscription to be here, extra storage costs money because it costs us money, and there's a small, limited amount of non-targeted advertising. Today, a paid email app we built funds the start. The full picture is on the transparency page.

Won't a future owner just sell out or change the rules?

There's no owner to sell, and no shares to buy. Surplus can't be paid out to insiders, the purpose is written into the founding documents, and a majority-independent board guards against quiet reversals. That's the whole reason for the nonprofit structure — a promise you can change on a whim isn't worth much.

Money & ads
Do you show ads?

Yes — a limited number, and not the kind you're used to. Sponsored posts are clearly labeled, capped at no more than one in every eight posts, and the same size as a normal post. There's no comment section on them, and the links go to clean destinations with no tracking attached.

The crucial part: we don't build a profile on you to target them. An advertiser can choose to appear alongside a topic — say, a gardening Circle — but they never see who you are, and we never follow you around to measure it. We only report aggregate, anonymous click totals. That's why we say "no tracking, no profiling, no selling you" rather than "no ads."

Do you sell or share my data?

No. We don't sell it, rent it, or share it with advertisers or data brokers. We collect as little as we can to run the service, and what we hold stays here. This isn't a policy we can quietly change — not selling you is the entire point of the organization.

Your data & the experience
Is there an algorithm deciding what I see?

No engagement-maximizing algorithm. Your feed shows posts from the people and groups you follow — not whatever an AI predicts will keep you scrolling or wind you up. The goal is a calm neighborhood, not a slot machine.

Do you use my content to train AI?

No. We don't harvest your posts or messages to train AI models. Any automated tools we use are narrow — for example, helping human moderators triage reports — and never a substitute for people making the real decisions.

What happens to my data if porchstroll shuts down?

It gets deleted — not sold. If porchstroll ever winds down, we'll give you clear notice and a window to export everything you've put here. After that, your data is deleted. It is not treated as an asset, it is not handed to creditors, and it is not passed to whoever might buy what's left.

On the money side: whatever funds remain after obligations are settled go to a mission-aligned nonprofit — privacy, digital rights, or community infrastructure. The non-distribution rule holds all the way to the end. Neither the founder nor anyone else here walks away with it. The full detail is on the governance page.

Private conversations are private — we don't proactively scan them. A conversation is only ever reviewed if someone inside it reports it, and then a real person looks, not an algorithm. We've also kept messaging text-only by design, which removes an entire category of abuse rather than trying to police it after the fact.

How is this different from Mastodon, MeWe, or the others?

The privacy-respecting options today tend to be either nonprofit-but-technical (hard for most people to use) or easy-but-for-profit (still answerable to owners or investors). porchstroll aims for the gap: a nonprofit, general-purpose, genuinely easy-to-use network. The structure and the simplicity together are the point.

Getting in & helping out
Can I join yet?

Not quite. We're pre-launch and opening the porch slowly. You can join the waitlist on the main site and we'll invite you in when there's room.

How can I support porchstroll?

Right now, the most direct way is to buy the email app once it's out — it funds the incorporation and legal groundwork. Joining the waitlist and telling a friend helps too. If formal donations open up later, we'll say so here.

Who's behind this?

porchstroll is founder-led for now, with an independent board forming. The clearest answer to "who" and "why" is in the founder's letter.
