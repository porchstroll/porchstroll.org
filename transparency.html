Transparency

Where the money comes from, where it goes, and what everyone gets paid.

As a taxable nonprofit rather than a 501(c)(3), porchstroll isn't required to publish much of anything. We're going to publish more than a charity would — not because we have to, but because trust on a platform like this is earned with specifics, not slogans.

What we'll publish, every year

An annual financial summary

Committed

A plain-language accounting of money in and money out — where revenue came from, what it cost to run porchstroll, and whether we ended the year in surplus or deficit. Written to be read, not to be hidden in a filing.

An independent CPA audit

Committed

An outside accountant verifies the numbers, so you're not just taking our word for them. The audit is published here alongside the summary.

Compensation, in the open

What everyone gets paid — including the founder.

Most organizations treat pay as the one number they'll never show you. We're doing the opposite, because how an organization pays people tells you what it actually values.

We commit to three things: a pay-ratio cap — a fixed limit on how much more the highest-paid person can earn than the lowest-paid; published salary bands, so you can see the range for every role; and the founder's salary named, not buried. The board serves unpaid. The exact figures are being finalized and will appear here once set.

Where the money comes from

Honest revenue, tied to real costs.

Individuals use porchstroll for free. Nonprofits and community groups do too. For-profit businesses pay a subscription, since they're here to reach people — and extra storage costs money because storage genuinely costs us money. Nothing is priced to punish you for being popular.

There is a limited amount of advertising: clearly labeled sponsored posts, capped at no more than one in every eight, the same size as a normal post, with no tracking and no profile built on you. The full detail is in the FAQ. And right now, the thing keeping the lights on is a small paid email app — a private mail client you buy once and own — whose proceeds fund getting the nonprofit off the ground.

Nothing to report yet — and we'd rather say so. porchstroll hasn't launched, so there are no annual numbers to publish. This page exists now so the commitment is on the record before the first dollar moves. The first financial summary and audit will appear after our first operating year.

How these commitments are protected is on the governance page. Why any of this exists is in the founder's letter.
