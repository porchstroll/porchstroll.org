porch etiquette

A short guide to how we treat each other here — written the way a neighbor would explain it, not a lawyer.

porchstroll is built around a simple idea: this is a neighborhood, and neighborhoods work because people treat each other with a basic level of decency. Porch Etiquette is how we put that into words.

The idea

Your porch is your space. Treat everyone else's the same way.

On a real street, you wouldn't stand on your porch and shout that your neighbor looks terrible, or that the business down the block is a rip-off. You wouldn't walk up to someone's porch and throw tomatoes at it just because you disagree with them.

The same principle applies here. Your Porch is yours — share what you care about, what you're working on, what you believe in. But how you show up on everyone else's matters just as much.

What this looks like in practice
🌱

Talk about yourself, not against others

Share your story, your cause, your work, your ideas. Politicians: tell us why you're running, not why your opponent is terrible. Businesses: tell us what you make and why you care. The porch is for showing up, not tearing down.

💬

Disagree with ideas, not with people

Debate is welcome. Honest disagreement is healthy. But there's a difference between "I see it differently, and here's why" and showing up on someone's Porch just to make them feel bad. One is a conversation. The other is just noise.

🚫

No attacks on how people look, who they are, or where they come from

Comments about someone's appearance, identity, background, or anything they didn't choose have no place here. Full stop. This isn't a close call.

🏪

Compete fairly, or don't compete here

If you're a business or organization on porchstroll, you're welcome to tell your story and make your case. You're not welcome to use this platform to run down competitors, spread rumors, or manufacture outrage about someone else. If your pitch requires tearing someone else apart, it's not a pitch — it's an attack.

🤝

Be a good guest on other people's porches

When you comment on someone else's post, you're visiting their space. Engage with what they said. Ask questions. Add something. Don't show up just to be unkind — that's not a visit, that's a disruption.

🔇

If you have nothing good to say, keep strolling

You don't have to comment on everything. If someone's post isn't for you, that's fine — keep strolling. The report button exists for things that genuinely cross a line, not for things you simply disagree with.

The spirit behind it

Think about an actual neighborhood street on a warm evening. People are on their porches. Some are chatting across the fence. A candidate is walking the block introducing themselves. A local shop owner is waving from their stoop.

Nobody is screaming insults. Nobody is following someone home just to argue. Nobody is throwing tomatoes at a neighbor's front door because they put up a yard sign they disagree with.

That's the neighborhood we're trying to build here. It doesn't mean everyone agrees — it means everyone shows up like a decent neighbor.

How this works

Community-first, human-reviewed

We don't have an algorithm scanning for rule-breakers. If something crosses a line, members can report it — and a real person will review it. We believe in human judgment, not automated enforcement.

For private Chat conversations, only someone in the conversation can report it. We don't surveil private messages. Privacy means privacy.

Repeated or serious violations will result in removal. But we're not looking to catch anyone out — we're looking to protect the neighborhood for everyone who's here in good faith.

porchstroll is a place to connect, not to be sold to — and not to be shouted at. We think most people already know how to be a good neighbor. This is just a reminder that the same rules apply here.

Welcome to the porch. We're glad you're here.
